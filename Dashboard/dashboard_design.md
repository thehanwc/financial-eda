# Dashboard Design

---

## Purpose

The Power BI dashboard is designed to **translate exploratory findings into business-ready insights**. It targets stakeholders who require **high-level performance visibility** while retaining the ability to drill down into key dimensions.

The dashboard prioritizes clarity, interpretability, and decision support over visual complexity.

---

## Design Principles

The dashboard adheres to the following principles:

- **Business Relevance** – Each visual answers a specific business question.
- **Information Hierarchy** – KPIs precede detailed breakdowns.
- **Consistency** – Uniform labeling, formatting, and visual structure.
- **Interactivity** – Filters and slicers enable focused analysis.

---

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

Primary KPIs displayed include:
- **Total Sales**
- **Total Profit**
- **Profit Margin (%)**
- **Units Sold**

These metrics provide an immediate overview of organizational performance.

---

## Visual Components

### Sales and Profit Trends
- Time-series visuals illustrating revenue and profit evolution
- Used to identify trends and seasonality

### Segment and Product Performance
- Comparative visuals showing contribution by segment and product
- Enables identification of top and bottom performers

### Geographic Performance
- Country-level breakdowns of sales and profit
- Supports regional performance evaluation

### Discount Impact Analysis
- Comparison of discount bands against sales and profitability
- Used to assess pricing and promotional effectiveness

---

## Business Questions Addressed

The dashboard is designed to answer:
- Which segments and products drive profitability?
- How do discounts affect revenue and margins?
- Which countries contribute most to overall performance?
- How does performance change over time?

---

## Intended Audience

This dashboard is suitable for:
- Business managers
- Sales and finance stakeholders
- Data analysts supporting decision-making

---

## Outcome

The Power BI dashboard serves as the **final communication layer** of the project, ensuring that analytical insights derived from EDA are **accessible, actionable, and aligned with business objectives**.
